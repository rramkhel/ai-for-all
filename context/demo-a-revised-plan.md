## The Big Idea

Demo A tells a single story about one fictional construction owner navigating real problems — and we scaffold his AI use from zero to expert across three scenarios. The audience watches "Steve" level up in real time, each scenario adding complexity and showing what's possible at the next tier.

The throughline: **the same documents, the same business, but progressively smarter use of AI.**

---

**Steve Makara** · Owner, Birchwood Builders Ltd.

## The Three Scenarios (Scaffolded Progression)

Each scenario layers on more AI competency. We're building a story arc: Steve starts skeptical, ends converted.

### Scenario 1 — "The Quick Answer" *(Beginner: Upload & Ask)*

**The Setup:**
> *"Steve just got a call from one of his guys on site. The worker wants to know: if he gets hurt on the job, what exactly is covered? Is it WCB? Is there an employer obligation beyond that? Steve's got the Alberta Employment Standards Code somewhere on his laptop, but it's 90+ pages. He doesn't have time to read it — he's got a client walk-through in 20 minutes."*

**What We Show:**
1. Open NotebookLM with **just one document** uploaded: Alberta Employment Standards Code
2. Ask: *"What are an employer's obligations when a construction worker is injured on the job in Alberta? What's covered under WCB vs. what I need to handle as the employer?"*
3. Point out the **citations** — "See those little numbers? Click one. It takes you right to the section. This isn't AI guessing — it's grounded in your document."

**Why This Scenario (Survey Data):**
- Construction respondent #1: "Help with routine tasks... Automate customer communications" + concern about "accuracy and fact checking"
- Construction respondent #5: "Automate and template some tasks... Email, sorting data" + concern about "security of my information"
- The citation feature directly addresses the #1 concern across construction respondents: **accuracy and trust**

**Say This:**
> *"Show of hands — who's ever had to read a government compliance document cover to cover? Right. Steve just got his answer in 8 seconds. And he can verify every word."*

---

### Scenario 2 — "The Deep Dive" *(Intermediate: Multiple Docs + Complex Queries)*

**The Setup:**
> *"Now it's Thursday evening. Steve's sitting at the kitchen table trying to respond to an RFP from the City of St. Albert. It's a small municipal renovation project — exactly his wheelhouse — but the RFP is 30 pages of requirements, insurance clauses, and safety compliance language he needs to cross-reference against the OH&S Act. Last time he bid on one of these, he spent an entire weekend on it. His wife is not impressed."*

**What We Show:**
1. Now we have **three documents** in the same notebook: Employment Standards Code + Alberta OH&S Act + a sample municipal RFP
2. Ask: *"Based on the RFP requirements and the OH&S Act, what safety certifications and documentation does Birchwood Builders need to have in place before submitting this bid? Summarize the requirements in a checklist I can hand to my site supervisor."*
3. Then: *"Draft an executive summary for our RFP response that addresses the safety compliance section. Reference specific OH&S requirements."*
4. Show how NotebookLM **cross-references across documents** — it pulls from the RFP requirements AND the OH&S Act simultaneously

**Why This Scenario (Survey Data):**
- Construction respondent #2: "administration and policy" + concern about "understanding and then implementation"
- Construction respondent #4: "Enhance our customer journey" + concern about being "over my head"
- RFP response is universally relatable for construction — and it's a task that eats entire weekends

**Say This:**
> *"Steve just did in 2 minutes what used to take him a full weekend. And notice — it didn't just summarize the RFP. It cross-referenced the safety requirements against the actual legislation. That's the power of giving AI multiple documents to work with."*

---

### Scenario 3 — "The Power User" *(Advanced: Full Notebook + Output Formats)*

**The Setup:**
> *"Steve's now a few weeks in. He's been uploading everything — his employee handbook, the city's building code amendments, his insurance policy summary, supplier agreements. His NotebookLM has become his compliance brain. But here's the real unlock..."*

**What We Show:**
1. Switch to **Jeff's fully built Construction NotebookLM**: [Jeff's Notebook](https://notebooklm.google.com/notebook/18779246-5c6d-4d96-89a7-5354f650e52e?authuser=1)
2. Walk through what a "mature" notebook looks like — multiple sources, organized by topic
3. Now show the **output formats** that go beyond Q&A:
   - **Audio Briefing:** *"Generate a podcast-style overview of the new overtime banking rules."* → Play 30 seconds. *"Steve listens to this on his drive between job sites."*
   - **Mind Map:** *"Create a visual map of all the safety compliance requirements across these documents."* → Great for understanding how regulations connect
   - **Study Guide / FAQ:** *"Generate an FAQ document I can give to new hires about their rights and obligations."* → Instant onboarding material
   - **Briefing Document:** *"Summarize everything in these documents that affects residential renovation projects specifically."* → Board-ready summary

**Why This Scenario (Survey Data):**
- Construction respondent #3: "Don't really know [what AI can do]. So far do not like the wording... It needs to learn more." → This is literally about giving AI more context
- Construction respondent #3 also: "Each industry is different, so hard to have general info to apply to all" → Jeff's notebook IS the industry-specific version
- Construction respondent #6: "Marketing properly and saving time with AI" + concern about "false information" → The citation system and document grounding directly solve this

**Say This:**
> *"This is the difference between asking a random stranger for advice and asking your own research assistant who's read every document your business has ever produced. Same engine. Completely different fuel."*

## The Key Contrast (Weave In, Don't Belabor)

At some natural point — probably during the transition from Scenario 1 to 2 — briefly show the difference:

1. **Generic LLM (no docs):** Ask ChatGPT/Gemini the same WCB question cold → you get a generic answer, possibly inaccurate for Alberta, no citations
2. **NotebookLM with 1 doc:** Same question → accurate, cited, specific to the actual legislation
3. **NotebookLM with full profile:** Same question → cross-referenced, contextualized to Steve's business

> *"This is what we mean by 'fuel.' Same engine, three totally different results."*

Don't dwell on this — 60 seconds max. The scenarios themselves demonstrate the progression more powerfully than a side-by-side comparison.
