## The Three Scenarios (Scaffolded Progression)

### Scenario 1 — "The Generic Trap" *(The Problem: AI without fuel)*

**The Setup:**
> *"Show of hands — who's tried using AI to write a social media post or marketing email? And how many of you thought the result sounded... like everyone else? Right. Let me show you why."*

**What We Show:**
1. Open a **blank ChatGPT conversation** — no project, no context, nothing uploaded
2. Type: *"Write a social media post promoting a kitchen renovation spring special for a renovation company."*
3. Let the generic result sit on screen for a beat. The audience needs to *recognize their own experience* in this moment.

**Why This Works (Survey Validation):**
This is literally what Concrete Options described: "do not like the wording it uses when we have tried for marketing." And Charlene Zoltenko Insurance: "Marketing, better use of language, not sure all yet it can do." You're validating their frustration before you solve it.

**Say This:**
> *"This is what most people do. And then they say 'AI doesn't work for marketing.' But the problem isn't the AI — the problem is you just asked a brand-new intern to write your marketing without telling them a single thing about your business. No brand voice, no pricing, no idea who your customers are. Of course it's generic."*

---

### Scenario 2 — "The 10-Minute Machine" *(Intermediate: Build the Project + The Contrast)*

**The Setup:**
> *"So what if we gave that intern a proper onboarding? What if we spent 10 minutes — once — handing them your recipe book? Watch what happens."*

**What We Show:**
1. **Switch to the pre-built ChatGPT Project** (already set up with Evergreen Renos docs)
2. Quickly show the project setup — uploaded docs (brand voice, pricing, best email), custom instructions. Don't read them out; just gesture: *"Three documents. One set of instructions. Took about 10 minutes."*
3. Type the **same prompt:** *"Write a social media post promoting our spring kitchen renovation special."*
4. **This is the money shot.** The output should use Evergreen's voice, their actual spring promo pricing, mention St. Albert, reference the warranty, sound like Dave and Maria.

**The Contrast (Let It Breathe):**
> *"Same AI. Same question. The only difference is the fuel. On the left, it's driving on empty. On the right, we gave it our brand guide, our pricing, and our best email. That's it."*

If the timing works, scroll back to the generic version for a visual side-by-side. If not, the audience already remembers it — the contrast is visceral.

**Why This Is the Core Moment (Survey Validation):**
- Concrete Options: "It needs to learn more" → We just taught it.
- Confections Cake Co: "handing tasks over to a program feels daunting" → We showed it's a 10-minute setup, not a massive project.
- DKI St. Albert: "Marketing properly and saving time with AI" → This IS marketing properly with AI.
- Charlene Zoltenko Insurance: "not sure all yet it can do" → Now she sees what it can do.

**Say This:**
> *"It knows the spring promo. It knows the price ranges. It mentions St. Albert. It's talking like Dave and Maria, not like a marketing textbook. You do this setup once, and every time you come back, it already knows your business."*

### Scenario 3 — "The Daily Driver" *(Advanced: Real Workflow Scenarios)*

**The Setup:**
> *"But this isn't just a social post machine. Once your project is set up, you can throw real business scenarios at it. Let me show you three things most of you told us you need."*

**What We Show — pick 2 of 3 depending on time:**

**Prompt A — The Follow-Up Email (Customer Retention):**
> *"Write a follow-up email for a homeowner who got a kitchen quote from us 2 weeks ago but hasn't booked. Be warm, not pushy. Mention that our spring booking calendar is filling up."*

Why: 4 construction respondents mentioned customer communications/journey as a challenge. Follow-up is the #1 thing that slips through the cracks for owner-operators.

**Prompt B — Seasonal Content Calendar (Local Tie-In):**
> *"Create a content calendar for Evergreen Renos for March through June. Include posts tied to Alberta spring (snow melt = renovation season), the St. Albert Lifestyle Expo, and Farmers' Market season starting in June. Mix of social posts, one email per month, and Google Business Profile updates."*

Why: This is where the St. Albert local knowledge hits. When the AI outputs content referencing the Lifestyle Expo and Farmers' Market, people in the room will think *"Wait — it knows about OUR events?"* That's because the brand voice doc mentions the service area, and the prompt provides the local context. It demonstrates how to get hyper-local output.

**Prompt C — Quick Fire Google Business Profile Posts:**
> *"Give me 5 Google Business Profile posts for this month. Short, casual, seasonal for Alberta late winter / early spring. Include one that mentions our 5-year warranty."*

Why: GBP is the most underused free marketing tool for local businesses. Most people in the room have a Google Business Profile and never post to it. Five posts in 8 seconds = their entire month handled.

**Say This (after showing results):**
> *"A follow-up email that sounds like Dave wrote it. A four-month content calendar tied to real St. Albert events. Five Google posts ready to copy-paste right now. That's what happens when your AI has been properly briefed."*

## Tool Swap Notes: ChatGPT Projects vs. Gemini Projects

The existing demo was built around Gemini Projects. Here's what changes for ChatGPT:

| Element | Gemini Projects | ChatGPT Projects |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| Where docs go | Upload as Google Docs | Upload files directly (PDF, Docs, images) |
| Custom instructions | Project settings → Instructions | Project settings → "Instructions" field |
| Interface | Google-native, ties to Drive | Standalone, most recognized AI brand |
| Cost | Free tier works, Advanced $25.99 CAD/mo | Free tier has Projects, Plus is $27 CAD/mo |
| Audience familiarity | Lower (many haven't tried Gemini) | Higher (ChatGPT is the name most people know) |

**What to prep differently:**
- Create a ChatGPT Project named "Evergreen Renos — Marketing"
- Upload the same 3 documents (brand voice, pricing, best email) as files
- Copy the same system instructions into the Project Instructions field
- Have a blank ChatGPT tab open separately for the generic contrast

**The LLM-Agnostic Close (keep this):**
> *"I showed you ChatGPT because it's the name most of you know. But this same approach works in Gemini Projects and Claude Projects. The key isn't which tool — it's the principle: give it your context, and it stops being generic. In your take-home toolkit, we've got setup instructions for all three."*
