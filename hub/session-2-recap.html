Session 2 Recap

Your 24/7 Marketing Intern — March 2026

What We Covered

🎯 Part 1: The ICP Brief (Rachel)

Every marketing strategy starts with one question: who are you actually trying to reach? We walked through the ICP Workshop Worksheet — a three-part exercise to identify your best customer, build a one-page profile card, and stress-test a real business decision against it. Then we looked at a completed example: Gillian George's Compassion Center ICP, showing how specific client language, dealbreakers, and decision-making triggers make AI-generated marketing dramatically more relevant.

🏪 Part 2: Google Business Profile (Jeff)

For many of your customers, your Google Business Profile IS your website. Jeff showed how to turn a neglected GBP into a working storefront — using Gemini to write a business description, generate weekly posts, and draft Q&A answers. The key stat: complete profiles are 2.7x more reputable and 18x more discoverable than incomplete ones. We also covered Pomelli as a next-level GBP management option.

🔬 Part 3: Deep Research (Rachel)

We revealed a full Growth Strategy Report for Compassion Center — competitive landscape, market trends, ICP refinements, and a 30/60/90 day action plan — all generated from a single Deep Research prompt. The report identified competitors Gillian didn't know about, surfaced sector-specific trends, and produced a week-by-week plan realistic for a solo entrepreneur with $0 budget and 2 hours per week. Then we showed you the exact prompt that created it, so you can do the same for your business.

📅 Part 4: The Content Calendar (Rachel)

This is where iterative AI workflows clicked. Instead of one magic prompt, we walked through a five-step process: (1) mine the research for trending themes, (2) pick your content mix (Educate / Relate / Offer / Prove), (3) generate 12–15 content ideas, (4) curate and write full posts with platform targeting and hashtags, (5) assemble everything into a four-week calendar. The result: a month of marketing written in the right voice, for the right customer, on the right platforms — built in about 20 minutes.

Key Takeaways

1
Your ICP is the fuel for everything. The Ideal Customer Profile isn't a one-time exercise — it's the document you paste into every AI conversation. The more specific it is (real language, real dealbreakers, real decision triggers), the better every piece of AI-generated content becomes.
2
Iterative beats one-shot. The five-step content calendar workflow proves it: research first, cherry-pick what matters, generate options, curate the best, then assemble. Each step builds on the last. One giant prompt can't match this.
3
Your Google Business Profile is your digital front door. If you don't have a website — or haven't updated yours in years — your GBP is what customers see first. A complete, active profile is 18x more discoverable than an incomplete one.
4
You're the Head Chef, not the intern. AI will write whatever you ask — including fake reviews and fictional customer stories presented as real. It has no conscience. You decide what's ethical. Always humanize and verify before publishing.

The 5-Step Content Calendar Process

This is the iterative workflow from Part 4. Each step builds on the last — that's why it works better than a single prompt.

1
Mine the Research
What You Do

Start in the same conversation as your deep research report.

What AI Does
"Pull out the top 8-10 trends most relevant to my business and my ideal customer. For each, explain why it would resonate with [ICP name]."
2
Pick Your Mix
What You Do

YOU choose 4-5 themes. Apply the content mix: Educate, Relate, Offer, Prove.

This is the human step

Don't skip it — your judgment matters here.

3
Generate Ideas
What You Do

Review the options AI gives you.

What AI Does
"Using themes [X, Y, Z], generate 12-15 content ideas. For each: working title, content mix category, format, and one-sentence hook."
4
Curate & Optimize
What You Do

YOU pick ~8 ideas you actually like.

What AI Does
"Take ideas [X, Y, Z]. Write full post copy in [voice], adapt for best platform, add hashtags, include a soft CTA, suggest posting time, flag repurposing opportunities."
5
Assemble the Calendar
What You Do

Review and humanize the final output.

What AI Does
"Organize into a 4-week calendar: Week, Day, Platform, Title, Post Copy (first 2 lines), CTA, Hashtags. 3-4 posts per week. Flag which can be batched vs. need a real photo."
