Session 2 Recap

Your 24/7 Marketing Intern — March 2026

What We Covered

🎯 Part 1: The ICP Brief

Every marketing strategy starts with one question: who are you actually trying to reach? We walked through the ICP Workshop Worksheet — a three-part exercise to identify your best customer, build a one-page profile card, and stress-test a real business decision against it. Then we looked at a completed example: Gillian George's Compassion Center, showing how specific client language, dealbreakers, and decision-making triggers turn generic AI outputs into targeted, relevant marketing.

🏪 Part 2: Google Business Profile + Pomelli

For many of your customers, your Google Business Profile IS your website. Jeff showed how to turn a neglected GBP into a working storefront using Gemini — writing a business description, generating weekly posts, and drafting Q&A answers in minutes. We also introduced Pomelli, Google's new AI tool that scans your website and generates on-brand marketing content automatically. The key stat: complete profiles are 2.7x more reputable and 18x more discoverable than incomplete ones.

🔬 Part 3: Deep Research

We revealed a full Growth Strategy Report for Compassion Center — competitive landscape, market trends, ICP refinements, and a 30/60/90-day action plan — all generated from a single Deep Research prompt. The report identified competitors Gillian didn't know about, surfaced sector-specific trends, and produced a week-by-week plan realistic for a solo entrepreneur with $0 budget and 2 hours per week. Then we showed you the exact prompt that created it, so you can do the same for your business.

📅 Part 4: The Content Calendar

This is where iterative AI workflows clicked. Instead of one magic prompt, we walked through a five-step process: mine the research for themes, pick your content mix, generate ideas, curate and write full posts, then assemble into a calendar. The result: a month of marketing written in Gillian's voice, targeted to her customer, optimized for the right platforms — built in about 20 minutes.

Key Takeaways

1
Your ICP is the fuel for everything. The Ideal Customer Profile isn't a one-time exercise — it's the document you paste into every AI conversation. The more specific it is (real language, real dealbreakers, real decision triggers), the better every piece of AI-generated content becomes.
2
Iterative beats one-shot. The five-step content calendar workflow proves it: research first, cherry-pick what matters, generate options, curate the best, then assemble. Each step builds on the last. One giant prompt can't match this.
3
Your Google Business Profile is your digital front door. If you don't have a website — or haven't updated yours in years — your GBP is what customers see first. A complete, active profile is 18x more discoverable than an incomplete one.
4
You're the Head Chef, not the intern. AI will write whatever you ask — including fake reviews and fictional customer stories presented as real. It has no conscience. You decide what's ethical. Always humanize and verify before publishing.

The 5-Step Content Calendar Process

This is the iterative workflow from Part 4. Each step builds on the last — that's why it works better than a single prompt. Do this in one AI conversation so context carries forward.

1
Mine the Research
What You Do

Start in the same conversation where your deep research report or ICP lives (or paste in key findings).

The Prompt
Based on the market research, pull out the top 8-10 trends, topics, or themes that are most relevant to my business and my ideal customer right now. For each one, give me a one-line explanation of why it would resonate with [ICP name].
2
Pick Your Mix
What You Do

This is the human decision step. Choose 4-5 themes from the list. Apply the content mix framework:

  • Educate — build trust ("here's how this works")
  • Relate — build connection ("you're not alone")
  • Offer — drive action ("here's how I can help")
  • Prove — build credibility ("here's what a client experienced")
This Is Your Call

No prompt here — this is YOUR decision. Don't skip it. Your judgment on what themes matter is what makes the final content relevant.

3
Generate Ideas
What You Do

Review the options AI gives you.

The Prompt
Using themes [X, Y, Z], generate 12-15 content ideas for [business name] for next month. For each idea, give me: a working title, which content mix category it fits (Educate / Relate / Offer / Prove), the format (social post, short blog, email, GBP post, carousel, or video concept), and one sentence describing the hook. Make sure ideas connect to [ICP name]'s specific pain points and language — not generic talk.
4
Curate & Write
What You Do

Pick ~8 ideas you actually like.

The Prompt
Take ideas [X, Y, Z]. For each one: write the full post copy in [your voice description], adapt for the best platform and explain why, add 5-8 relevant hashtags, include a clear but soft call-to-action, suggest a posting day and time based on when [ICP name] is most likely scrolling, and flag where an idea could be repurposed across platforms.
5
Assemble the Calendar
What You Do

Review and humanize — add your real photos, tweak anything that doesn't sound like you.

The Prompt
Organize all of this into a content calendar for the next 4 weeks. Format as a table with columns: Week, Day, Platform, Content Title, Post Copy (first 2 lines), CTA, and Hashtags. Space it out so I'm posting 3-4 times per week across all platforms. Flag which posts can be batched and scheduled in advance vs. which ones need a real photo or a timely edit.
