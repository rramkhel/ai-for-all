AI Safety Guardrails

The Red/Yellow/Green framework for keeping your business data safe when using AI tools.

Think of it Like a Traffic Light

Before sharing anything with an AI tool, ask yourself: "What category does this information fall into?" This simple system helps you avoid costly mistakes.

🛑

RED

NEVER Share

Highly sensitive data that should never go into AI systems.

⚠️

YELLOW

Anonymize First

Useful data, but remove identifying details before sharing.

✅

GREEN

Safe to Share

General information that poses no risk.

RED Never Share with AI

This data is too sensitive. Even if the AI promises confidentiality, these systems store and learn from inputs. A data breach could be catastrophic.

💳

Financial credentials — Credit card numbers, bank account details, payment information

🔐

Access credentials — Passwords, API keys, security codes, PINs

🏥

Health information — Medical records, health conditions, prescription details

📋

Government IDs — Social Insurance Numbers, driver's license numbers, passport info

📜

Confidential contracts — NDAs, proprietary agreements, legal documents under privilege

💬

Private communications — Customer emails/texts without consent, internal HR matters

YELLOW Anonymize Before Sharing

This information can be valuable for AI help, but needs to be scrubbed of identifying details first. Remove names, specific amounts, and anything that could identify individuals.

👤

Customer details — Names, contact info, addresses → "Customer A" or "a client in Edmonton"

💰

Financial figures — Exact revenue, specific quotes → "approximately $X" or "in the $50-75K range"

👥

Employee info — Names, performance details → "a team member" or "an employee in sales"

📊

Business metrics — Specific KPIs others could identify → use ranges or percentages

✏️ Example: Anonymizing a Customer Scenario

Before: "I need to write a follow-up email to John Smith at ABC Corp (john@abc.com) about his $47,500 renovation project on 123 Main Street."

After: "I need to write a follow-up email to a customer about their mid-range residential renovation project (kitchen and bathroom)."

GREEN Safe to Share

General, non-sensitive information that poses no risk. This is where AI can help most freely!

📝

General templates — Email formats, document structures, standard processes

🏢

Public business info — What you do, services offered, general capabilities

📚

Industry knowledge — Best practices, common challenges, standard terminology

💡

Ideas & concepts — Marketing concepts, business strategies, improvement ideas

🌐

Public content — Published blog posts, social media content, marketing materials

❓

General questions — "How do I..." questions about processes or techniques

🎯 Test Your Knowledge

📧 Scenario 1: Writing a Quote

"I want AI to help me write a quote for Sarah Johnson's bathroom renovation at 456 Oak Avenue. The total is $32,750."
YELLOW — Anonymize first!
Instead say: "Help me write a quote for a residential bathroom renovation, mid-range budget around $30-35K."

💼 Scenario 2: Marketing Help

"Can you help me write a social media post about the services my plumbing company offers?"
GREEN — Safe to share!
General business descriptions and marketing content are fine. Go ahead!

🏦 Scenario 3: Invoice Processing

"Here's a scan of my customer's credit card to process a payment..."
RED — Never share!
Financial credentials should never go into AI systems. Use your secure payment processor instead.

📞 Scenario 4: Follow-up Call Script

"Write me a follow-up call script for leads who requested quotes but haven't responded."
GREEN — Safe to share!
You're asking for a general template, not sharing any specific customer data. Perfect use of AI!

📋 Quick Reference Card

🛑 Red — Never

Passwords, credit cards, SINs, health records, confidential contracts

⚠️ Yellow — Anonymize

Names, addresses, specific $ amounts, employee details

✅ Green — Go

Templates, public info, general questions, ideas

💡 Remember

When in doubt, anonymize. It only takes a few seconds to remove identifying details, and it could save you from a serious privacy incident. Ally is programmed to remind you about these guardrails—but the final check is always on you.
