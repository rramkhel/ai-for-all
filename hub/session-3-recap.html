Session 3: Killing the Admin Drudgery

Your admin task toolkit — prompts, tools, and quick wins organized by pain point.

Small business owners spend 16 hours per week on administrative tasks. That's two full workdays every week spent on email, scheduling, invoices, and paperwork instead of serving customers or growing your business.

We've organized the most common admin time-sinks into four tiers, with a free and paid solution for each. Click any task to see the exact prompt or tool instructions.

Quick Guide: Claude vs. ChatGPT vs. Gemini

If you... Best free option Why
Use Gmail / Google Calendar Gemini Built right into your inbox and calendar — no switching apps
Need to analyze long documents (contracts, policies, regulations) Claude Handles massive documents, best at nuanced comprehension
Want to process images (receipts, whiteboards, handwritten notes) ChatGPT or Gemini mobile Strong image-to-text on both; Gemini mobile is great for on-the-go photos
Want a persistent business assistant that remembers context Any — use Projects or Gems All three offer this; pick the one you're most comfortable with
Want AI connected to your real email/files/calendar Claude or Gemini Claude connects via MCP; Gemini is natively embedded in Workspace
Want to search your own business documents NotebookLM Purpose-built for document research — upload and ask questions

Don't overthink the choice. Pick one, try it for a week. You can always switch later. They're all free to start.

Auto-sort incoming email so you see what matters first

▾
Setup Instructions (Gmail)
Create filters to auto-label and sort:

1. In Gmail, click the search bar dropdown (right side)

2. Enter criteria (e.g., "from:newsletter@" or "subject:invoice")

3. Click "Create filter" → Choose action (Skip Inbox, Apply label, etc.)

Recommended filters:

  • All newsletters → Skip inbox, label "Read Later"
  • Key clients (by email domain) → Label "Priority"
  • "Unsubscribe" in body → Label "Newsletters"
Email Scheduling & Snoozing

Free: Gmail / Outlook built-in Paid: Boomerang ($5/mo)
▾
Setup Instructions (Gmail)
Schedule Send:

When composing an email, click the dropdown arrow next to "Send" → "Schedule send" → Pick date/time.

Snooze:

Hover over an email → Click the clock icon → Choose when to be reminded. The email disappears and returns to inbox at that time.

Pro tip: Batch your email. Write replies Sunday night, schedule them for Monday 8am so you don't look like you work weekends.

Grant & Funding Application Assistant
Generate a first draft of grant applications, RFP responses, or government forms in minutes
Free: ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini Works for: Grants, RFPs, government forms, loan applications
▾
The Prompt
I need help drafting a grant application. Here's what I have: **About the grant/funding program:** [PASTE THE GRANT CRITERIA, ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS, AND EVALUATION CRITERIA HERE — you can usually find these on the funder's website] **About our organization:** - Organization name: [NAME] - What we do: [DESCRIBE IN 2-3 SENTENCES — e.g., "We're a seniors activity centre that provides social, recreational, and support programs for adults 50+ in our community"] - Location: [CITY, PROVINCE] - Years in operation: [NUMBER] - People we serve: [WHO — e.g., "approximately 800 members, primarily seniors aged 55-85"] - Key programs/services: [LIST YOUR MAIN OFFERINGS — e.g., "fitness classes, meal delivery partnerships, systems navigation for government services, educational talks, social events, facility rentals"] - Team: [SIZE AND STRUCTURE — e.g., "3 full-time staff, 1 part-time, 40+ active volunteers, 9-member board of directors"] - Annual budget: [APPROXIMATE — or say "prefer not to share" and I'll draft without it] - Previous grants received: [ANY RELEVANT ONES — or "first time applying"] **What we want to fund:** [DESCRIBE THE SPECIFIC PROJECT OR NEED — e.g., "We want to expand our meal delivery program to reach isolated seniors who can't access the centre"] **Draft a first-pass application that:** 1. Addresses every criterion from the grant requirements 2. Uses our ACTUAL programs and impact — not generic statements 3. Includes specific, concrete examples wherever possible 4. Identifies where I'll need to fill in real numbers or data I haven't provided 5. Matches the tone expected by the funder (professional but community-focused) 6. Flags any eligibility concerns or weak areas I should strengthen before submitting **Important:** Mark anything you've estimated or assumed with [VERIFY] so I know what to fact-check. This is a first draft — I'll review and refine before submitting.
How to Use It
  1. Find the grant criteria on the funder's website and paste it in
  2. Fill in your organization details — be specific about your programs
  3. Describe the project or need you want funded
  4. Review the draft, verify any [VERIFY] tags, and refine before submitting
Why This Works
Grant applications follow predictable structures. AI can map your real programs to the funder's criteria — but you still verify facts and add the human nuance. This takes a weekend project down to an hour of refinement.

🧰 The Admin Diagnostic

Not sure where to start? This conversational prompt helps you identify your biggest admin time-sinks. Paste it into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini and have a conversation.

I want you to help me identify my biggest administrative time-wasters and match them to AI tools that can help. Let's have a conversation. First, I'll tell you about my business: - Business type: [What you do] - Team size: [Just you? Or employees?] - Main services/products: [Brief description] Then ask me these questions one at a time (wait for my answer before the next): 1. "Walk me through a typical day. What tasks eat up most of your time outside of actually serving customers?" 2. "What's one task you dread doing every week? The one you procrastinate on?" 3. "Think about the last time you stayed late or worked a weekend. What were you catching up on?" 4. "What questions do customers ask you repeatedly? And where does that communication happen - email, phone, in person?" 5. "If you could magically eliminate one admin task forever, what would it be?" After I answer all five, give me: 1. My top 3 admin time-wasters ranked by impact 2. For each one: a free tool or AI prompt that could help 3. The one thing I should try THIS WEEK (the quick win) Keep your recommendations practical — assume I have 30 minutes to try something new, not a whole weekend.

Tip: The more specific your answers, the better the recommendations. "Email" is too vague — "writing follow-up emails to leads who didn't book" is actionable.
