Session 1 Recap

Stop Guessing, Start Growing — February 24, 2026

What We Covered

🔍 Demo A: The Research Assistant (NotebookLM)

We showed how a construction business owner (Steve from Birchwood Builders) used Google's NotebookLM to make sense of the 90+ page Alberta Employment Standards Code. Instead of reading the whole document, Steve uploaded it and asked specific questions — getting instant, cited answers. The key insight: AI that's grounded in YOUR documents gives you reliable answers, not guesses.

📊 Demo B: AI-Assisted Spreadsheets

Jeff showed how AI can help with the spreadsheet tasks that eat up your evenings — organizing data, writing formulas, and making sense of numbers. The takeaway: you don't need to be a spreadsheet expert when AI can help you get the same results faster.

📣 Demo C: Your Marketing Assistant (ChatGPT Projects)

We built a marketing assistant for a fictional renovation company (Evergreen Renos) by uploading a brand voice guide, pricing sheet, and a top-performing email into a ChatGPT Project. The result: AI that writes social posts, emails, and content calendars that actually sound like your business — not generic robot text.

Key Takeaways

1
AI needs fuel (your context) to be useful. Generic AI gives generic answers. Upload your documents, describe your business, and the output transforms.
2
The Red/Yellow/Green framework keeps you safe. Not everything should go into an AI tool. Passwords and financial credentials = never. Customer names = anonymize first. General business info = go ahead.
3
You don't need to be technical. Every tool we showed works with plain English questions. If you can describe your problem, AI can help.
4
Pick one tool and start. Don't try to learn everything at once. Choose ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, set up Ally, and try one prompt from your Sector Pack this week.
