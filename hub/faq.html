Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the toolkit and using AI for your business.

Getting Started

Which AI tool should I pick — ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini?

Whichever you already have an account on, or whichever feels most comfortable. ChatGPT is the most popular and has the most tutorials online. Claude is excellent for writing tasks. Gemini works especially well if you already use Google Workspace (Gmail, Google Docs).

All three work with Ally. You can always switch later — nothing is locked in.

Is the free tier really enough?

Yes, for most small business use cases. The free tiers of all three platforms give you plenty of conversations per day. You'd only need to upgrade if you're using AI heavily throughout the day or want access to the newest, most powerful models.

Our advice: start free and only upgrade if you find yourself hitting limits.

What if the access code doesn't work?

Make sure you're typing it in all capital letters with no spaces: AIFORALL2026

If it still doesn't work, try a different browser or clear your browser's stored data (Settings → Clear Browsing Data → Cookies). Still stuck? Email info@stalbertchamber.com and we'll get you sorted.

I'm not great with technology. Can I really do this?

Absolutely. Every tool in this toolkit uses plain English — no coding, no technical jargon. If you can type a question or fill out a form, you can use these tools.

Start with the Business Profile (it's just a fill-in-the-blank form) and take it one step at a time.

Using Ally

What should I ask Ally first?

Try something real, not a test. Good first questions:

"What's taking up most of my time this week?"
"Help me write a follow-up email to a client."
"What are some quick wins I could try for my marketing?"

The more specific you are, the better Ally's answers will be.

Can my team or employees use Ally too?

Yes! Share the Ally Setup instructions and have each person fill out their own Business Profile with their role and responsibilities. Each person sets up Ally in their own AI account.

It's a great way to keep everyone consistent.

What if my business information changes?

Just update your Business Profile (re-fill the template in the toolkit) and then re-paste the updated profile into your AI platform — go to wherever you originally set up the instructions and replace the old profile section with the new one.

Ally gave me a weird or wrong answer. What should I do?

AI isn't perfect — it can sometimes misunderstand or give off-target responses. Try rephrasing your question with more detail and context.

Using the structured prompts from the Sector Packs tends to give more reliable results than freeform questions. And always use your own judgment — Ally is an assistant, not a decision-maker.

Safety & Privacy

Is it safe to use AI for my business?

Yes, if you follow the Red/Yellow/Green framework we teach in the workshops. The short version:

Never share: passwords, financial credentials, or personal identification numbers (SIN, etc.)
Anonymize first: customer names and specific dollar amounts
Safe to share: general business information, templates, and ideas

Review the full framework on the AI Safety Guardrails page.

Does the AI remember or store what I type?

AI platforms process your input to generate responses, and their policies on data retention vary. That's exactly why we teach the guardrails — treat AI like a very helpful stranger.

Share information you'd be comfortable putting on a bulletin board, and anonymize anything private. Check each platform's privacy policy for specifics.

What was that Samsung story from the workshop?

In 2023, Samsung employees accidentally pasted proprietary source code into ChatGPT during their work. The code was then processed by the system and could have been exposed through the model's training data. Samsung ended up banning ChatGPT internally.

This is why we teach the Red/Yellow/Green framework — so you always pause and consider what you're sharing before you hit Enter.
